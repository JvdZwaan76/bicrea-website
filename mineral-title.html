Mineral title services

Mineral Title Reports

Comprehensive analysis of mineral ownership under a single tract or designated unit. Each report establishes current ownership, traces the chain of title, identifies encumbrances, and flags exceptions or open issues that warrant attention.

  • Chain of title from sovereignty (or stated starting point)
  • Current mineral ownership with fractional interests
  • Active leases, assignments, and encumbrances
  • Open title questions with curative recommendations
  • Source documentation index

Chain-of-Title Verification

Targeted research to verify or reconstruct chain of title for a specific transaction or curative purpose. Used when a full report is not required but a particular link in the chain needs to be established or challenged.

  • Specific link verification with source documents
  • Reconstruction of broken or unclear chains
  • Conflicting-claim analysis
  • Documented findings with evidentiary support

Runsheets

Chronological abstracts of every recorded instrument affecting a tract, suitable as the foundation for an attorney's drilling title opinion or as a standalone reference for transactional work.

  • Every recorded instrument indexed chronologically
  • Grantor / grantee / instrument type / recording data
  • Reservation and exception language captured verbatim where material
  • Cross-references to source images

Acquisition Due Diligence

Mineral rights due diligence supporting acquisition, divestiture, financing, or unitization. Findings packaged for review by counsel, banks, or boards with risk-rated commentary on every flagged issue.

  • Title status across the entire deal package
  • Risk-rated issue list with curative cost estimates
  • Lease status, term, and obligations
  • Production allocation analysis where relevant

Curative Title Work

Identification and resolution of mineral title defects. Common curative work includes missing heirs, scrivener errors, broken chains of title, recordation gaps, and conflicting reservations. Coordinated with counsel for any actions requiring legal authority.

  • Missing heir research and locate
  • Affidavit of heirship preparation support
  • Corrective deed coordination
  • Quiet title support documentation

Florida Mineral Rights

Florida is home to a smaller mineral title market than Texas or Oklahoma, but the issues — phosphate rights, severed estates, MRTA implications — are real and specialized. We have particular depth on Florida-specific mineral and surface-rights interaction.

  • Florida MRTA (Marketable Record Title Act) analysis
  • Phosphate and aggregate rights research
  • Severed estate analysis under Florida law
  • Florida-specific recording quirks and counties

Mineral title questions

A mineral title report is a documented analysis of who owns the mineral interests under a particular tract of land, how those interests have changed hands over time, and what encumbrances or exceptions affect them.

Unlike a surface title report, it focuses specifically on severed mineral estates — oil, gas, coal, hard-rock minerals — and traces ownership through deeds, probate records, and instruments of conveyance, often back to the original sovereignty patent.

Standard reports on a single tract are typically delivered in 7 to 14 business days from confirmed engagement. Complex chains, multi-tract assemblies, or curative-heavy projects are quoted on a case-by-case basis with a specific timeline included in the engagement letter.
