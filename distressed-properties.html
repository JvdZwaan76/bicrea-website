Who We Help

When the regular real estate path doesn't fit

Listing a property with an agent works well — when the property is move-in ready and the seller can wait for a retail buyer with financing. When that's not the case, the regular path can become its own kind of trap. That's where we come in.

We buy houses, condos, vacant land, and small commercial properties throughout Florida that have one or more of the following: open code enforcement cases, recorded liens, complicated title, structural issues, vacancy or abandonment, problematic tenants, inherited or probate complications, or simply more deferred maintenance than a typical buyer would accept. We do not require you to fix anything, clean anything, or even stage the property. We do not require you to deal with showings or financing contingencies.

In return, our offer reflects the cost and risk we are taking on. It will typically be below what you would get from a fully renovated retail listing — because we are taking the renovation, the negotiation with the city, the lien resolution, and the time onto our side of the deal. Whether that trade-off works for you depends on your situation. The only way to know is to look at the numbers, take them to someone you trust, and decide.

Situations We Handle

If any of this sounds like your property, we should talk

Code Enforcement Violations

Daily fines accumulating, repeat notices, threatened condemnation. We negotiate with the city and assume the cured cost.

Recorded Liens

Tax liens, contractor liens, judgment liens, HOA liens — we work to address them as part of the transaction.

Probate and Inherited Property

Inherited a property you don't want to maintain or fix up? We work with executors and heirs through the probate timeline.

Out-of-State Owners

You inherited a Florida property or have a rental you can't easily manage from another state. We close remotely.

Title Problems

Clouded title, missing heirs, unclear ownership history, recordation gaps. We pursue curative work as part of the deal.

Structural or Major Repair Issues

Foundation, roof, plumbing, electrical, mold, hurricane damage. The kind of problem that scares retail buyers and their inspectors.

Vacant or Abandoned

Empty property accumulating issues — vandalism, squatters, code citations. We can take it off your hands quickly.

Problematic Tenants

Non-paying tenants, lease disputes, holdover issues. We can buy with tenants in place and handle the situation ourselves.

Pre-Foreclosure

Active foreclosure cases require special handling under Florida law. We follow it strictly and recommend independent counsel.

Tax Delinquency and Tax Liens

Unpaid property taxes, tax liens, or properties heading toward tax deed sale — we resolve as part of our purchase.

Hoarder and Cleanout Situations

Heavy contents, deferred maintenance over years. You don't have to clean it out — we handle that part.

Divorce or Hardship Sales

Life events that need a clean, quick, predictable sale rather than the unpredictability of the open market.

Don't see your situation? Call us anyway. We've handled most things at least once, and if we can't help, we often know someone who can.

Consumer Protection

Your protection is built in

Florida has specific consumer protection laws governing distressed property and foreclosure-related transactions, including the Florida Foreclosure Rescue Fraud Prevention Act (Fla. Stat. §501.1377) and Florida Statutes Chapter 501. We follow them strictly. That means:

  • Every offer is presented in writing, with the terms in plain language and required statutory disclosures included where applicable.
  • You have the right to consult independent legal, tax, and real estate counsel before signing — and we encourage you to do so. Always.
  • Where state law provides a cancellation right (such as in foreclosure-related transactions), it is honored fully and stated clearly in the contract.
  • We do not ask for any payment, deed transfer, or signature before you have had reasonable time to review the offer with anyone you choose.
  • We make no representations about saving you from foreclosure, restoring credit, or guaranteeing outcomes that depend on third parties or future events.

BICREA is not your attorney, accountant, or financial advisor. The information on this page is general and is not legal advice. Your specific situation may involve different rules and we encourage you to seek your own counsel before making any decision regarding your property.
